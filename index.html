Frequently asked questions

The things people ask us most before booking. Don't see your question? Send a message and we'll reply within one business day.

Do you offer a free initial consultation?

Yes. The first 15-minute call is free — we'll discuss your situation and give you a flat-fee quote before any work starts.

I just moved to Canada — can you still file my taxes?

Absolutely. Newcomer returns are one of our specialties. Bring your SIN, any Canadian income slips, and details of foreign income for the part of the year you arrived. We'll handle the rest.

What documents do I need as a student?

At minimum, your T2202 (from your school), any T4 slips from part-time or co-op work, and receipts for moving or transit expenses if relevant. We'll send you a tailored checklist after our first chat.

How does pension income splitting actually work?

Eligible pension income (RRIF withdrawals, registered pension plans) can be split with a spouse to balance taxable income across both returns. We model the split and pick the optimal amount — most clients save several hundred to a few thousand dollars.

How long does CRA take to process my refund?

Returns we file electronically are usually assessed within 2 weeks. With direct deposit, refunds typically land 1–2 business days after your Notice of Assessment.

Can you help if CRA is reviewing or auditing my return?

Yes — even if we didn't prepare the original return. We'll review the request, gather supporting documents, and respond to CRA on your behalf.
